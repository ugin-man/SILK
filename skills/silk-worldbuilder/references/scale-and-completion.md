# Scale and completion

## What grandeur means

Grandeur is not a large word count or a long list of proper nouns. A grand world supports movement across scale, time, place, social position, interpretation, and subject matter while preserving recognizable structure.

SILK V1 targets a substantial first-edition world corpus. It cannot manufacture the decades of authorship, play, publication, and cultural reinterpretation behind D&D or the Cthulhu Mythos in one model pass. It can build the internally coherent foundation from which that accumulation can grow.

Treat named franchises as scale and experience benchmarks, not templates. Match the requested density, navigability, historical layering, mystery, and generative capacity without imitating their protected expression or importing their taxonomies by default.

## Scope envelope

Define completion relative to an explicit envelope in `world.yaml`:

- spatial reach: site, region, network, continent, planet, planes, or another topology;
- temporal reach: present slice, centuries, eras, cycles, nonlinear time, or timeless structure;
- social reach: selected communities, multiple strata, civilizations, nonhuman societies, or other actors;
- ontological reach: ordinary reality, magic, divinity, cosmic entities, dream, afterlife, or world-specific domains;
- resolution target: which subjects must be load-bearing and which may remain texture or mystery.

The envelope prevents both premature completion and infinite expansion. A planetary world does not require every town, but it requires representative and structurally important coverage across its promised reach.

## Scale axes

Inspect only axes relevant to the world, but do not let one axis substitute for all others:

- **temporal:** formation, transformations, forgotten periods, residues, present instability;
- **spatial:** centers, edges, routes, barriers, local variation, absent zones;
- **social:** rulers, institutions, workers, families, outsiders, marginalized and nonhuman perspectives;
- **material:** food, shelter, labor, bodies, tools, infrastructure, transport, waste, scarcity;
- **institutional:** formal rules, informal practice, enforcement, corruption, adaptation, failure;
- **epistemic:** official accounts, local knowledge, expert models, religious claims, disputed evidence, unknowns;
- **ontological:** ordinary and extraordinary kinds of existence without forcing them into one system;
- **causal:** multiple roots, delayed effects, feedback, accidents, unintended consequences, boundaries;
- **experiential:** grandeur, familiarity, terror, beauty, wonder, humor, intimacy, or other intended appeals at varied intensity.

Do not require equal treatment. Allocate depth by load-bearing importance and intended experience.

## Zoom test

A spherical world remains intelligible at several zoom levels:

- world-scale pressures;
- region or network;
- polity, ecology, institution, or culture;
- community, site, household, or practice;
- person, object, ritual moment, or ordinary decision.

The lower levels demonstrate consequences; they are not protagonist plots. A world fails the zoom test when global claims have no lived manifestation or local details have no larger conditions.

## Maturity promotion gates

### Level 0 to 1

- intended experience and boundaries are explicit;
- the seed can support more than one vertical direction;
- obvious scope mismatch is resolved;
- hypotheses remain distinguishable from canon.

### Level 1 to 2

- load-bearing subjects and independent causal roots are selected;
- dynamic collections describe actual content;
- no stock category is mandatory;
- primary vertical packets have explicit depth questions;
- the initial premise does not own every primary subject.

### Level 2 to 3

- primary systems have mechanisms, limits, variation, history, lived effects, and edge-case review as relevant;
- important histories alter the present;
- accidental sibling templates and trait inheritance are removed;
- major scope promotions have accepted obligations;
- weak load-bearing concepts have been replaced rather than cosmetically repaired.

### Level 3 to 4

- vertical threads form meaningful junctions across domains and scales;
- overconnections and false unifications are cut;
- the relation topology has clusters, bridges, boundaries, and independent roots;
- regions, eras, and social layers are more than names;
- premise gravity and semantic saturation are within the world's intended design;
- important subjects pass interest and structural review.

## Final completion dimensions

Every dimension requires evidence from canonical subject IDs, relation IDs, junctions, views, and audit decisions.

- `subject_integrity`: one canonical file per subject; identity-defining information is not fragmented and duplicate identities are not hidden behind different IDs or titles.
- `depth`: load-bearing vertical packets are integrated at required maturity.
- `interest`: intended appeal is realized; weak primary concepts are not preserved by sunk cost.
- `causality`: major present states have traceable causes, transformations, and residues.
- `weave`: accepted relations create meaningful effects; likely false relations are cut.
- `breadth`: multiple scales, places, times, and perspectives exist where relevant.
- `independence`: the world contains independent causal roots and non-effect zones.
- `anti_template`: shared structures have in-world causes; accidental copies are repaired.
- `scope`: local claims have not silently become global or ontological laws.
- `naming`: names form a readable ecology and do not substitute for content.
- `coherence`: claims, scales, registries, views, and overview agree, and the world has a recognizable signature without collapsing independent roots into one cause.
- `mystery`: deliberate unknowns create value; accidental omissions do not hide as mystery.
- `plot_boundary`: world states exist without chapter, scene, protagonist, quest, or ending design.
- `usability`: a reader can enter through overview and catalog, distinguish approval and knowledge states, understand one subject from its canonical file, follow meaningful related items, and submit an expansion as a hypothesis without bypassing canon controls.

## Clean audits

Require two clean audits of different integrated states. An audit is clean only when it discovers no new critical gap, not when existing queue items were closed. The second audit must occur after integrating all first-audit changes and must vary its route through the world.

Audit one emphasizes topology and system interactions. The other emphasizes reader entry, subject integrity, local consequences, interest, naming, and boundaries. Rewording the same inspection does not count.

Follow `audit-protocol.md` and record counterevidence for every passing dimension.

## Completion blockers

Do not complete while any of these remain:

- critical work, replacement, contradiction, relation, or hypothesis decision;
- load-bearing subject below required maturity;
- unresolved rescue cascade;
- unexplained global distribution or ontological promotion;
- generic quality claims without evidence;
- key subject understandable only by reading fragmented auxiliary files;
- primary concept judged `deepen`, `rescope`, `merge`, `replace`, or `discard`;
- one premise unintentionally explaining most of the world;
- an unreviewed complex system capable of invalidating economy, war, law, ecology, or daily life;
- structural validator failure;
- only one clean audit.

## Honest delivery

Structural and theoretical completion does not prove human-perceived greatness. Deliver the reports and state which claims are supported by artifact evidence and which require user evaluation or later V3 story accumulation.

The automatic endpoint is `internally_complete`. `user_approved` is a later authority decision, not another quality score. Generate the review packet and preserve generated subjects as unreviewed until the user acts.
