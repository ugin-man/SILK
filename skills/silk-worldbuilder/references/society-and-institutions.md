# Society and institutions lens

Use for polities, law, economy, military, organizations, family systems, education, health, religion as institution, and other organized social processes.

## Formation and purpose

Distinguish founding purpose, current function, public justification, and who actually benefits. Institutions can survive after their original purpose declines.

## Authority and capacity

Separate claimed jurisdiction, legitimate authority, information, resources, enforcement capacity, informal practice, and local compliance. A state is not uniformly present across its territory.

## Decision path

Trace how important decisions are initiated, interpreted, delayed, funded, enforced, evaded, and appealed. Do not invent identical councils, ministries, guilds, or military branches for every polity.

## Material base

Inspect labor, extraction, production, storage, transport, taxation, debt, property, care work, and scarcity as relevant. An economy is not only named currencies and trade goods.

## Social reproduction

Inspect how membership, family, inheritance, education, status, office, skill, belief, and exclusion persist or change across generations. Avoid treating culture or species as socially uniform.

## Conflict and cooperation

Not every institution needs internal betrayal or moral ambiguity. Inspect ordinary cooperation, competence, trust, negotiated practice, failure, disagreement, and adaptation according to intended appeal.

## Law and reality

Separate written rule, enforcement, customary settlement, privilege, local exception, and lived expectation. Magical or divine law may still be interpreted through institutions unless the world establishes direct automatic enforcement.

## Military and security

Inspect recruitment, training, supply, command, replacement, intelligence, mobility, fortification, doctrine, political control, and noncombat roles. A special order needs a non-interchangeable function, not merely stronger equipment.

## Institutional diffusion

Shared structures across regions require conquest, imitation, common ancestry, trade, religious authority, professional networks, colonial inheritance, convergent pressure, or another cause. Category similarity is not enough.

## Exit evidence

An institution is deep enough when its real operation, capacity, limits, material support, historical change, local variation, and lived encounters are understandable without filling a generic organization template.
