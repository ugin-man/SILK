# Phase contracts

## Spin

Inputs are the active gap, current level, promotion blockers, intended appeal, scope envelope, relevant registry summaries, boundaries, and canon locks.

Outputs are structurally different hypotheses, an explicit `no new subject` candidate when plausible, scope and obligation estimates, one selected candidate or a reframed gap, and only instructive rejected hypotheses. Spin cannot write directly into approved canon.

## Deepen

Inputs are the selected subject, weight, required maturity, chosen lenses, direct claims and relations, known scope promotions, and stress risks.

Outputs are vertical packet answers, unknowns classified as deliberate mystery or work, proposed canonical revisions, interaction and weave candidates, queued obligations, and a depth exit decision. Deepen cannot close with only classifications, names, spell lists, dates, offices, or powers.

## Weave

Inputs are a developed vertical packet, direct neighbor summaries, relation and non-relation ledgers, and saturation and scope information.

Outputs are reviewed relations, effects on each subject, junction packets, rejected likely relations, affected canonical files, and topology findings. Weave cannot accept a relation solely to satisfy link count or thematic similarity.

## Challenge

Inputs are user intent, canon locks, the artifact as written, direct context, and scope, maturity, similarity, and dependency summaries.

Outputs are artifact-first interest and structural verdicts, anti-convergence and rescue-debt findings, one action among keep, deepen, rescope, merge, replace, discard, and cut, plus impact analysis before destructive action. Write the verdict before repair proposals.

## Integrate

Create one coherent change set:

```yaml
id: change.iteration-018.magic-boundaries
subjects_create: []
subjects_update: []
subjects_move: []
subjects_discard: []
relations_add: []
relations_update: []
relations_cut: []
claims_update: []
collections_update: []
queues_close: []
queues_open: []
reports_rewrite: []
maturity_changes: []
completion_update: null
manifest_update: null
```

Update canonical files, subject registry, relation registry, claim registry, taxonomy, scope and maturity ledgers, queues, decision and iteration logs, generated views, reports, `completion.yaml`, `state.yaml`, and `manifest.yaml` as affected.

Keep at most one active change set. If interrupted, preserve it as unapplied or partially applied with exact remaining operations; do not open a second integration set until the first is reconciled.

Do not leave a moved subject at its old path, a cut relation in a subject summary, a replacement target with stale dependencies, or a generated view that presents old canon.

## Promotion review

After integration, inspect the level gate. Record evidence, blockers, regressions, and next packet. Promotion is a decision, not the default next step.

## Destructive impact analysis

Before merge, replacement, discard, scope contraction, or relation cut:

1. list direct subject and relation dependents;
2. identify claims relying on the old material;
3. identify views and reports summarizing it;
4. separate user canon from generated dependents;
5. choose rewrite, detach, preserve as disputed history, or discard for each dependent;
6. reopen maturity gates that relied on removed evidence.
