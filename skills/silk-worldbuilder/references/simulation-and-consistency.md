# Simulation and consistency

## Purpose

Stress load-bearing claims at plausible scales. Do not demand mundane realism from fantasy; demand that the world acknowledges consequences of its own rules.

## Orders of magnitude

When relevant, estimate or bound:

- travel and communication time;
- population and settlement support;
- food, water, fuel, shelter, and waste;
- military supply, replacement, training, and command;
- magical or technological throughput;
- disease, injury, healing, reproduction, and mortality;
- production, extraction, transport, storage, and trade;
- institutional reach and enforcement delay;
- creature range, habitat, migration, and reproduction;
- time needed for cultural, linguistic, ecological, or legal change.

Exact numbers are optional. Contradictory orders of magnitude are not.

## Capability stress

For any powerful capability, inspect:

- individual use;
- repeated use;
- coordinated mass use;
- adversarial use;
- criminal use;
- state or institutional use;
- infrastructure use;
- use by a different species or ontology;
- failure under scarcity, distance, fatigue, conflict, or bad information;
- second-order adaptation by opponents and ordinary people.

If magic heals, transports, creates food, binds names, predicts events, raises the dead, changes weather, or transmits information, ask why obvious applications have or have not transformed society. Accept local limits, cultural refusal, institutional capture, genuine rarity, technical barriers, unintended effects, or other causes only when they arise from the system rather than patching a hole.

## Interaction matrix

Do not test every possible pair. Select combinations capable of changing load-bearing assumptions:

```yaml
interaction:
  systems:
    - world.subject.weather_magic
    - world.subject.grain_tax
  question: Can coordinated rainmaking eliminate harvest risk and destabilize taxation?
  result: <integrated conclusion or explicit non-relation>
  obligations: []
```

Prioritize high reach, high frequency, high reversibility cost, and strategic incentives.

## Counterfactual probes

Select probes capable of falsifying important assumptions:

- the capability becomes common rather than rare;
- access is monopolized by a state, guild, species, family, or infrastructure;
- an opponent uses the same capability strategically;
- a low-skill use is repeated at massive scale;
- the capability stops for a generation;
- an isolated region gains access;
- a supposedly universal system does not operate somewhere;
- the inhabitants' taxonomy is wrong;
- a limit is bypassed indirectly rather than broken;
- several ordinary uses combine into an extraordinary consequence;
- institutions adapt competently instead of remaining passive;
- users refuse the obvious benefit for reasons already present in the world.

Do not canonize every probe. Use them to reveal missing consequences, false limits, and scope errors.

## Spatial consistency

Maintain routes, barriers, distances, environmental transitions, distribution limits, and travel assumptions. A global species, empire, language, or cult needs mechanisms that cross space. A supposedly isolated region needs barriers that survive known travel and communication.

## Temporal consistency

Track relative order, duration, overlapping eras, delayed effects, institutional memory, generational turnover, and material residues. Different cultures may use different calendars. Preserve uncertainty where dates are disputed.

## Institutional adaptation

Institutions are neither perfectly rational nor frozen. Inspect incentives, information, path dependence, vested interests, local variation, delay, corruption, legitimacy, informal practice, and failure. Do not assume every obvious use is adopted immediately or every old institution disappears when its original purpose declines.

## Stress outcome

Choose:

- integrate the consequence;
- narrow the claim;
- add a justified limit;
- separate phenomena previously unified;
- record a non-relation;
- replace the capability;
- preserve a contradiction as an intentional crisis.

Never hide a failed stress test by adding arbitrary exceptions without reviewing rescue debt.
