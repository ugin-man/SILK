# Coherence without convergence

## Balance

A world fails when every subject derives from one premise. It also fails when independent subjects feel imported from unrelated settings. Build cohesion through several kinds of fabric without forcing one universal cause.

## Sources of cohesion

- causal interaction;
- shared space, routes, barriers, or ecology;
- historical contact, inheritance, conquest, migration, or exchange;
- material dependence and infrastructure;
- institutional or linguistic transmission;
- recurring human or nonhuman questions;
- compatible intended appeals and tonal range;
- motifs that echo without being the same mechanism;
- consequences of living under the same broad world conditions.

Do not use every source. Select what the world establishes.

## Theme is not ontology

A world can repeatedly explore names, memory, belonging, winter, debt, transformation, or another question without making that theme a universal supernatural substance. Thematic resonance does not require a relation record or shared causal root.

## Independent roots can meet

Develop vertical threads independently, then inspect credible points of contact. A trade network, war, translation tradition, pilgrimage route, ecological boundary, or shared institution can make different roots part of one world without erasing their origins.

## World signature

Maintain a compact signature in the shape report:

- intended appeals;
- load-bearing questions;
- characteristic scales and rhythms;
- important material or historical conditions;
- forms of cohesion used;
- forms of independence protected;
- tonal range rather than one uniform tone.

The signature guides comparison but grants no properties to subjects.

## Two failure tests

### Over-converged

Remove the central premise. If nearly every subject loses its reason to exist, independent roots are insufficient unless total convergence was explicitly intended.

### Anthology soup

Move a major vertical thread to a different setting. If no history, relation, material condition, interpretation, or lived practice needs to change, integration is insufficient.

Repair over-convergence by rescoping and independent derivation. Repair anthology soup by meaningful contact and shared conditions, not by rewriting every origin into one myth.
