# Audit protocol

## Separation from generation

Audit an integrated state, not an active draft. Close the generation or repair packet, apply its change set, refresh views and reports, then begin audit from the artifact. Do not count the Challenge phase inside the same packet as a clean world audit.

One agent performs both routes, but uses different questions and evidence paths. Do not reuse the first audit wording or conclusions as proof in the second.

## Route A: Systems and topology

Inspect:

- canonical subject integrity;
- load-bearing maturity;
- honesty of subject weights and attempts to hide difficult systems below load-bearing status;
- claim scope and promotion obligations;
- capability stress and orders of magnitude;
- chronology, spatial distribution, logistics, and institutional adaptation;
- relation effects, junctions, boundaries, and supernodes;
- accidental templates, shared-trait causes, and premise gravity;
- unresolved contradictions, replacements, and rescue cascades.

Try to falsify the world's operating assumptions. Follow dependency edges and high-reach capabilities.

## Route B: Cold reader and world curator

Enter through overview, catalog, status view, and selected canonical pages without using generation rationale.

Inspect:

- whether the intended experience is actually present;
- whether major subjects deserve attention and remain understandable alone;
- whether related items are meaningful and navigable;
- whether naming is readable and varied;
- whether multiple zoom levels and perspectives make the world feel inhabited;
- whether breadth is real rather than a list of names;
- whether `not_relevant` coverage decisions honestly preserve the promised envelope;
- whether mystery is intentional and unfinished work is visible;
- whether user approval and provisional material are distinguishable;
- whether plot material has contaminated worldbuilding.

Try to find boredom, abstraction, confusion, repetition, overexplanation, and inaccessible structure.

## Counterevidence

Every passing dimension records:

- evidence supporting pass;
- strongest counterexample inspected;
- why it does not remain critical;
- affected IDs and paths.

An audit that only gathers confirming evidence is not clean.

## Clean decision

Record new critical gaps, resolved gaps, reopened gates, route, integrated-state identifier, and clean verdict. Any new critical gap resets the streak.

The second clean audit must inspect the state produced after all first-audit findings were integrated. If the first audit found no changes, the second route must still use a distinct entry path and counterevidence set.

## Meta-critic failure

Reject the audit itself when it:

- relies on self-assigned numeric scores;
- cites plans rather than artifacts;
- treats missing evidence as mystery;
- excuses weak material because fixes are possible;
- confuses coherent with interesting;
- confuses familiar with boring;
- confuses complexity with depth;
- treats validator success as quality proof;
- calls internally accepted material user-approved.
