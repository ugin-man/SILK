# Lived world and story affordance

## Purpose

Make large systems perceptible in ordinary choices without writing plots. A world feels grand when global structures and small lives coexist.

## Lived consequence

For each load-bearing system, sample relevant manifestations in:

- work and skill;
- food, shelter, clothing, and tools;
- travel and communication;
- family, inheritance, age, and care;
- education and access to knowledge;
- law, enforcement, evasion, and informal settlement;
- illness, death, burial, and memory;
- status, exclusion, obligation, and belonging;
- celebrations, routines, inconveniences, jokes, and taboos;
- local adaptation and misuse.

Do not mechanically fill all domains. Choose consequences that reveal the system and record meaningful non-effects.

## Avoid premise decoration

Do not rename every job, meal, bureau, or custom after the central premise. Ordinary continuity is part of the world. A magical society may still use plain names and solve many problems without magic.

## Reusable pressures

Create conditions that can support many stories without prescribing one:

- incompatible obligations;
- contested resources or jurisdictions;
- incomplete knowledge;
- institutional delay;
- changing environment;
- uneven access to power;
- historical residue;
- local practice conflicting with official rule;
- cooperation under pressure;
- stable arrangements that different people experience differently.

Do not select a protagonist, sequence scenes, assign a quest, or decide an ending.

## Perspective sample

Inspect how the same subject appears to several affected positions. Do not require every possible demographic category. Select positions that reveal different mechanisms, benefits, burdens, misunderstandings, or emotional registers.

## Texture versus load-bearing lore

Texture can remain concise when it demonstrates lived reality. Do not force every meal or household object into an independent canonical subject. Integrate small details into the subject they illuminate unless they have independent identity and relations.
