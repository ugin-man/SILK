# Deepening engine

## Vertical packet rule

Use a vertical packet to understand one load-bearing subject. Select relevant lenses; never fill every lens mechanically. Integrate accepted material into the canonical file and close the packet.

## Universal lenses

Consider these questions when relevant:

- **identity:** what is this, and what is it not?
- **intended appeal:** what experience should it create?
- **ontology:** what kind of thing is it inside the world?
- **formation:** how did it come to exist?
- **mechanism:** what actually makes it work or persist?
- **access:** who can use, enter, influence, inherit, or understand it?
- **limits:** what can it not do, reach, survive, or explain?
- **variation:** which differences are meaningful, and why?
- **failure:** how does it break, decay, misfire, or become obsolete?
- **history:** how has it changed rather than merely existed?
- **institutionalization:** who formalized, regulated, exploited, or resisted it?
- **lived reality:** how does it alter ordinary choices and material life?
- **interpretation:** who disagrees about what it means?
- **interactions:** what happens at contact with other load-bearing systems?
- **edge cases:** what plausible use or exploit threatens current assumptions?
- **future pressure:** what remains unstable now?

Depth is not the number of headings. Depth is the ability to answer consequential questions without generic filler.

## Recursive decomposition

When a vertical packet reveals a large subsystem, apply the canonical-subject test:

- keep it as a facet when it exists mainly to explain the parent;
- create a child subject when it has independent identity, mechanism or lifecycle, and meaningful relations beyond the parent;
- create a relation or claim instead when the information is primarily an interaction or disputed proposition;
- keep the parent canonical page self-sufficient with an overview of the decomposed subsystem.

Do not create one file per attribute, level, spell, office, era, or doctrine automatically. Do not keep an unreadable monolith merely to satisfy one-file integrity; one-file integrity applies per independently identifiable subject.

## Magic and extraordinary systems

When magic is load-bearing, inspect:

- whether magic is one ontology or a human umbrella term;
- primitive operations rather than only spell lists;
- whether attributes are real, taught, political, or mistaken;
- whether ranks measure power, complexity, risk, permission, or institutional curriculum;
- how combined effects emerge, interfere, or fail;
- whether divine, draconic, fae, ancestral, technological, or environmental phenomena share a substrate;
- who can learn, inherit, borrow, steal, survive, or misunderstand access;
- capability boundaries, scale limits, costs, failure modes, and irreversible effects;
- effects on food, health, labor, transport, communication, war, law, class, crime, and knowledge only where causally justified;
- why obvious large-scale solutions have not erased existing problems;
- regional schools, historical paradigms, suppressed models, and unexplained cases;
- repeated-use, mass-use, adversarial-use, and infrastructure-level consequences.

Do not assume elemental attributes, numbered ranks, mana, spell schools, universal casting, or stronger-combination logic. They are hypotheses, not defaults.

## Polities and institutions

Inspect formation, legitimacy, actual decision paths, resource extraction, enforcement limits, regional variation, informal power, succession or continuity, historical reforms, failures, external dependencies, and how ordinary people encounter the institution. Do not clone ministries, councils, military orders, or social classes across polities without a shared cause.

## Religions and transcendent subjects

Separate deity, doctrine, institution, ritual, believer experience, outsider interpretation, material practice, and historical change when they are genuinely distinct. Keep a deity's own identity in one canonical file. Do not require domains, laws, realms, clergy, or retinues for every transcendent subject.

## Species, creatures, and ecologies

Inspect habitat, distribution, limiting factors, reproduction or persistence, behavior, food or energy relationships, adaptation, interaction with settlements, local variation, and absence zones. Global distribution requires a diffusion mechanism. Being a monster is not a diffusion mechanism.

## Artifacts and technologies

Inspect whether the subject is unique, replicated, broken, dormant, misunderstood, owned, contested, personified, disposable, infrastructural, or culturally repurposed. A previous personified artifact does not establish personhood as an artifact trait. Shared construction features require a shared maker, process, lineage, or explicit historical transfer.

## Places and regions

Inspect physical formation, movement and access, resources and constraints, settlement patterns, local memory, external perception, internal differences, historical layers, and what does not apply there. Similar climates do not require similar metaphysics.

## Histories and transformations

Develop causes, actors, contingencies, contemporary interpretations, immediate consequences, later reinterpretations, material residues, institutions created or destroyed, populations moved, and unresolved present pressure. Avoid decorative timelines whose events leave no trace.

## Cultures, languages, and practices

Inspect internal variation, transmission, class and regional differences, material conditions, contact and borrowing, contested identity, historical change, ordinary enactment, and outsider labels. Do not make every cultural practice a direct symbol of the central premise.

## Exit criteria

Close a vertical packet when the subject's important role, operating logic, constraints, history, lived consequences, and unresolved pressure are understood to the depth demanded by its weight. Leave deliberate mystery only when the world benefits from uncertainty and record what remains known.
