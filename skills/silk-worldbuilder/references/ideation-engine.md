# Ideation engine

## Purpose

Create adjacent possibilities without allowing the first successful association to colonize the world. Possibility generation is broad; canon admission is narrow.

## Begin from a gap

Every ideation packet states:

- the observed world gap;
- why the gap matters at the current maturity level;
- the intended appeal or function;
- the desired scope;
- existing subjects that may constrain but must not template the answer.

Do not generate a deity because a gods folder exists or an artifact because an artifacts list is short.

## Existing-subject search

Before proposing a new subject ID, search registry titles, aliases, one-sentence roles, causal roots, scope, pattern tags, and direct relations. Ask whether the gap is:

- missing depth in an existing canonical subject;
- a missing relation or junction;
- a disputed claim;
- a local example belonging inside another page;
- a genuinely independent subject.

Prefer deepening, weaving, or merging when independent identity is absent. Different wording and title do not prove a new subject.

## Produce structurally different hypotheses

Generate candidates through several reasoning directions:

- **inheritance:** an existing cause genuinely extends here;
- **mutation:** the cause changes under new conditions;
- **independence:** a separate cause produces the new subject;
- **contradiction:** inhabitants or evidence reject the existing model;
- **absence:** the expected phenomenon does not apply here.

Do not canonize one candidate from each direction. Use the directions to escape first-association lock-in.

Do not generate only from previously approved examples or taste patterns. Include plausible surprise while respecting explicit exclusions and canon locks.

## Separate observation, model, and reality

For complex systems, distinguish:

- what inhabitants observe;
- how different cultures classify it;
- what institutions teach;
- what the world actually establishes, if knowable;
- what remains unresolved.

Attributes, levels, schools, divine power, dragon abilities, and fairy phenomena may be one system, overlapping systems, incompatible interpretations, or unrelated phenomena. Do not place them into a shared grid merely because inhabitants call all of them magic.

## Question synthesis operators

Generate `might` questions by changing one meaningful dimension at a time, then combine only high-impact dimensions:

- **actor:** who or what else can cause, access, resist, misunderstand, or embody it?
- **composition:** what happens when operations, attributes, users, or systems interact?
- **scale:** what changes from one use to repeated, collective, institutional, or infrastructural use?
- **environment:** where does it fail, mutate, disappear, or become ordinary?
- **time:** how was it understood or used before, and what can become obsolete?
- **knowledge:** is the classification true, political, educational, religious, or mistaken?
- **permission:** is difficulty actually power, precision, law, initiation, relationship, or access?
- **absence:** what if the expected connection does not exist?

Examples of question shape:

- Might high difficulty come from simultaneous constraints rather than combined attributes?
- Might divine phenomena be outside the human magic taxonomy rather than its highest rank?
- Might dragons alter local law by existing rather than casting spells?
- Might fae action be a biological or relational process humans misname as magic?
- Might the southern polar region have no soul relation despite northern funerary ice?

Do not answer every generated question or build a Cartesian product. Rank by load-bearing impact, ability to falsify assumptions, intended appeal, and current coverage debt.

## Hypothesis admission

Before canonization, inspect:

- intended-role fit;
- imaginative appeal;
- scope and blast radius;
- explanation debt;
- overlap with existing subjects;
- capacity for meaningful development;
- risk of premise convergence;
- whether no new subject is the better answer.

Store rejected candidates briefly when their rejection teaches the world. Do not fill the discarded archive with every transient thought.

## Claim promotion

Never promote a local claim into a world law automatically:

```yaml
claim: Ice used by the northern funerary rite preserves a soul trace.
scope: northern_funerary_rite
not_established_for:
  - natural_glaciers
  - polar_regions
  - ice deities
  - ice creatures
  - preservation magic
promotion_requires:
  - independent evidence
  - explicit world-level value
  - accepted downstream obligations
```

The promotion decision must list new consequences and files requiring revision. If that cost does not improve the world enough, retain local scope.

## Independence seeding

At every maturity promotion, inspect whether all important subjects descend from the initial premise. If so, open an independence packet based on geography, material conditions, historical accident, language, ecology, migration, social practice, or another plausible source. Independence is not random trivia; it is a second causal root capable of later intersection.
