# Geography and ecology lens

## Geography is constraint and possibility

Develop terrain, climate, routes, barriers, resources, hazards, settlement, and movement as interacting conditions. Do not paint a map with themed regions lacking causal structure.

## Formation and scale

Establish the processes forming important features at a useful level of precision. Fantastical geography may violate mundane physics, but its own persistence, boundaries, and consequences still need explanation or intentional mystery.

## Routes and barriers

Track movement of people, creatures, goods, information, armies, disease, beliefs, and technologies. A barrier is meaningful only relative to known capabilities. An isolated region must remain isolated against actual travel and communication systems.

## Ecological relationships

For load-bearing organisms or nonhuman entities, inspect energy or resource sources, habitat, reproduction or persistence, predation and competition, adaptation, disturbance, seasonal behavior, migration, extinction, domestication, and settlement response as relevant.

Do not require mundane biology from beings with different ontology. Require a stable account of how they persist and affect their surroundings.

## Distribution

Separate unique, site-bound, local, regional, migratory, dispersed, global, and ontological reach. Explain spread and limiting factors. Record absence zones likely to be overwritten by later generation.

## Regional identity

Derive a region from interacting conditions and historical experience, not one adjective, species, element, or commodity. Preserve internal variation, routes, edge zones, and disagreement about boundaries.

## Similar environments

Similar climate does not imply similar culture, magic, ecology, or spiritual meaning. Derive each region before comparison. Neither copy nor mechanically invert the first region.

## Map obligations

Keep relative position, travel assumptions, environmental transitions, river or current direction, resource movement, political reach, and creature distribution compatible. Exact cartography is optional in V1, but contradictory space is not.

When spatial placement becomes stable enough to visualize, record it through [spatial-registry.md](spatial-registry.md). Do not place a non-spatial subject such as a deity, law, or magical principle directly on a map. Place its spatial manifestation, jurisdiction, site, route, distribution, or influence area and link that feature to the canonical subject.

Geometry may remain `provisional` while relative constraints are still being tested. Do not promote attractive generated coastlines, borders, or coordinates into canon without causal and consistency review.

## Exit evidence

Geography is deep enough when important places constrain and enable life, routes and absences make sense under world capabilities, ecological distributions have causes, and regions remain more than themed labels.
