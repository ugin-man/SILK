# Red-team scenarios

Use these as design threats, not as content templates or proof that a world passed.

## Deep but tiny

The world has three excellent canonical subjects and marks every other coverage cell irrelevant. Reopen Coverage Planning, challenge the scope envelope, sample excluded cells, and reject Level 4 if promised breadth is absent.

## Broad but copied

The world has hundreds of files, but every polity has the same council and every artifact the same personhood. Use name-blind comparison, shared-cause review, Pattern Ledger, and replacement or merge. File count is counterevidence, not proof.

## Local law becomes ontology

A regional naming ritual becomes proof that all names have power. Return the claim to local scope unless independent evidence and accepted world-level obligations justify promotion.

## First creature colonizes ecology

Goblin-like creatures are global, so griffins and other monsters become global. Reset sibling assumptions. Derive distribution, propagation, habitat, and absence independently.

## One personified artifact defines the class

Later artifacts inherit speech, desire, and owner rejection. Require a shared maker or process; otherwise redesign each from its own purpose and state.

## Magic table without system

The corpus contains attributes, ranks, spells, and costs but cannot explain primitive operation, access, mass use, institutional adoption, or exceptions. Reopen subject Level 2 and use System Design Lens.

## Powerful solution ignored

Healing, transport, food creation, or prediction should transform society, but the world remains conventionally medieval. Run capability stress. Integrate adaptation, justify limits, separate phenomena, or replace the capability.

## One premise explains everything

Religion, geography, institutions, names, food, creatures, and history all derive from dragons, memory, ice, or another seed. Rescope claims, cut links, restore non-effect zones, and develop independent roots.

## Anti-convergence creates anthology soup

Every region is independent and none would change if moved to another setting. Build credible contact and shared conditions without rewriting all origins into one cause.

## Critic forces cleverness

Every familiar subject gains a tragic cost, secret inversion, internal conspiracy, and moral ambiguity. Recalibrate to intended appeal, preserve direct competence and grandeur, and restore compositional rhythm.

## Mystery hides missing work

Mechanisms and history are absent but labeled unknowable. Require consequences, evidence, competing interpretations, stakes, and protected boundaries or return the gap to Deepen.

## Canon fragmentation

A deity's origin, powers, worship, and historical change occupy separate facet files. Merge identity-defining material into one canonical file; keep independent events and institutions separate with summaries and relations.

## Relation inflation

Every co-located or thematically similar subject receives links. Cut relations without observable effects, preserve important non-relations, and reject link-density metrics.

## Replacement leaves ghosts

A discarded concept remains in relations, claims, overview, and coverage evidence. Run destructive impact analysis and one coherent change set; reopen maturity gates using removed evidence.

## Internal acceptance impersonates user approval

Generated subjects move to approved after internal audit. Return them to pending, set `internally_complete`, regenerate status views, and issue the user review packet.

## Character becomes plot

An important agent gains chapters, a quest, a climactic defeat, and a resolved arc. Rewrite as current goals, constraints, plans, relationships, possible developments, and open world pressure.

## Context exhaustion becomes completion

The agent stops after a large batch because the context is long. Persist state, active change set, next packet, and blockers; resume rather than claiming completion.
