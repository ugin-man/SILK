# Anti-convergence and precedent firewall

## Failure modes

Detect:

- **precedent leakage:** the first subject becomes the category template;
- **trait inheritance:** category membership is treated as a cause;
- **distribution creep:** one global creature makes later creatures global;
- **semantic monopoly:** one motif explains religion, geography, politics, history, and daily life;
- **premise gravity:** nearly every major subject descends from the initial idea;
- **mechanical contrast:** later subjects are generated as simple opposites;
- **surface variation:** names, species, elements, colors, weapons, or regions change while structure stays the same;
- **convenient unification:** distinct phenomena are forced into one elegant system without sufficient evidence.

## No inheritance by default

When creating a sibling subject, begin from its world role and cause, not from an existing file. Category membership grants no distribution, agency, ontology, ownership, lifecycle, hierarchy, powers, social role, or file sections.

Shared traits require an explicit common cause:

```yaml
shared_trait: personhood
shared_with:
  - world.subject.mourning_blade
cause: The same maker divided one memory across both artifacts.
```

Intentional similarity is allowed for manufactured series, related species, copied institutions, shared descent, convergent adaptation, or cultural transmission. Record the cause and meaningful variation.

Audit the shared cause itself. It should have evidence, timing, propagation path, limitations, and consequences beyond excusing similarity. A newly invented ancient maker, lost empire, common ancestor, or universal law whose main purpose is to save copied subjects is a rescue cascade, not a valid cause.

## Scope ledger

Important claims state their reach:

- `unique`
- `site_bound`
- `local`
- `regional`
- `migratory`
- `dispersed`
- `global`
- `ontological`

Global or ontological scope requires a mechanism, evidence, downstream obligation review, and explicit promotion. Convenience is not a mechanism.

These are comparison hints, not a closed enum. A world may require relational, bloodline-bound, dream-bound, language-bound, temporal-directional, network-bound, perception-bound, or other scope. Define custom scope by inclusion, exclusion, propagation mechanism, and boundary conditions.

## Distribution audit

For creatures, practices, technologies, institutions, and beliefs, inspect presence, absence, movement, reproduction or transmission, habitat or support requirements, barriers, historical spread, local adaptation, and extinction or rejection. Record meaningful absence zones.

## Sibling comparison

Compare subjects without names. Inspect:

- reason for existence;
- origin;
- ontology;
- distribution;
- agency;
- ownership or authority;
- lifecycle and present state;
- limitation and failure;
- relation to ordinary life;
- temporal behavior;
- structural role.

If they remain interchangeable, determine whether shared structure has an in-world cause. If not, redesign, merge, replace, or discard. Do not force every axis to differ; difference without cause is another template.

## Semantic saturation audit

Track major motifs and causal roots. Ask:

- Is a local association being repeated as a universal essence?
- Are new regions interpretations of old regions rather than independently derived places?
- Are unrelated systems being explained with the same metaphor?
- Does the world still contain domains where the central premise has weak or no influence?
- Are there independent histories that later intersect rather than originate together?

When saturation is found, prefer scope reduction, relation cutting, independent derivation, or replacement over adding decorative exceptions.

## Causal root ledger

Track each load-bearing causal root, why it is independent, subjects substantially derived from it, later intersections, scope, and whether it has become a semantic monopoly. A root may be geography, historical accident, world law, migration, ecology, institution, technology, language, or an unknown world-specific cause.

Do not count renamed consequences as independent roots. Do not require a fixed number; require enough independence for the promised envelope and intended world shape.

A decorative root created only to satisfy the audit is not enough. It needs its own vertical development, scope, consequences or protected non-effect, and credible later intersections where relevant.

## Do not enforce randomness

Anti-convergence does not mean maximum difference. Coherence needs recurrence, inheritance, and recognizable patterns. Preserve repetition with cause; remove repetition caused by the model's convenience.

## Pattern memory

Update `pattern_ledger.yaml` after detecting or accepting a repeated form. Record accidental generation habits to avoid, intentional recurrences and their causes, and comparison axes that exposed copying. Consult it before creating sibling subjects.
