# Documentation quality

## Canonical page contract

A canonical page should let a reader understand the subject without assembling its identity from auxiliary files.

Open with a concise identity and current significance. Then use only subject-relevant sections. Typical material includes present state, formation, operating logic, limitations, historical changes, interpretations, lived consequences, major relationships, and intentional unknowns.

Do not create empty standardized sections or repeat frontmatter in prose.

## Concrete before exhaustive

Use concrete practices, observable effects, decisions, objects, places, language, or incidents to make abstract systems perceptible. Do not mistake a list of classifications, powers, dates, offices, or names for depth.

Examples demonstrate rules but do not become mandatory templates for every sibling subject.

## Evocative without becoming fiction

Use selected sensory conditions, material objects, gestures, rituals, local phrases, visible consequences, and practical decisions so readers can imagine the subject. Do not replace explanation with decorative atmosphere, and do not turn the canonical page into a dramatized scene or chapter.

Technical precision and evocative presence can coexist. Match density and voice to intended appeal; cosmic uncertainty, civic procedure, creature ecology, and household practice need not sound identical.

## Related items

End important pages with related subjects grouped or ordered by meaningful relation. Include one-line relation summaries. Do not provide a bare ID dump.

```markdown
## Related subjects

- [Coastal Crown](<resolved-relative-path>) (`world.subject.coastal_crown`) — uses the deity's silence to preserve disputed legitimacy.
- [First Withdrawal](<resolved-relative-path>) (`world.subject.first_withdrawal`) — transformed worship and created the present shoreline law.
```

Generate the Markdown path from `subject_registry.yaml`. The stable ID is authoritative; the resolved path makes raw GitHub and local Markdown navigation usable. Regenerate inbound related-item links after status moves, reclassification, rename, merge, split, or replacement.

## Avoid lore fragmentation

Do not move detail into a separate page merely because the page is long. Split when the new subject has independent identity and relations. Summarize the split material in the original page.

## Detail ownership

Give a detailed fact, mechanism, or event one natural canonical owner. Other subject pages summarize only the consequence needed to understand themselves and link to the owner. Do not duplicate full historical accounts, rule explanations, or biographies across several canonical pages.

Self-sufficient means the reader understands this subject's relationship to the detail, not that every related detail is copied in full.

## Avoid generated-document monotony

Vary organization according to subject. Do not make every page use the same number of sections, the same cadence, identical conflict language, or a repeated formula of origin, cost, secret, and current crisis.

Use tables for comparison, chronology for sequences, lists for inventories, and prose for explanation. Do not force one format everywhere.

## Names

Approved or internally accepted pages use one canonical title. Aliases require actual usage differences. Do not leave option lists such as A/B/C/D in canon.

## World overview

The overview explains the world's intended experience, shape, major roots, transformations, boundaries, and navigation. It does not summarize every subject or repeat the entire corpus.

## Reader usability

Check that a new reader can distinguish canon, unreviewed material, disputed in-world claims, deliberate mystery, and discarded design material. Avoid presenting process notes as lore.
