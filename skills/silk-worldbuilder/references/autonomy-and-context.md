# Autonomy and context control

## One request, many internal cycles

Treat the user's request as authorization to continue through internal maturity levels until completion or a genuine creative blocker. Do not stop after scaffolding, one phase cycle, one promotion, or a convenient batch size.

## State as external memory

Maintain `state.yaml` as the resume point:

```yaml
world_level: 2
current_phase: deepen
active_packet: magic_foundation
active_change_set: null
last_integrated_iteration: 17
next_action: resolve capability boundaries before weaving into warfare
promotion_blockers:
  - magic_foundation lacks mass-use consequences
```

Use files instead of relying on conversational memory. After every integration phase, update manifest, state, completion, registries, queues, maturity ledger, decision log, generated views, and compact reports as affected.

## Context loading

Load only:

- `world.yaml` and `state.yaml`;
- relevant registry rows;
- active queue item and packet;
- affected canonical subjects and direct relations;
- the required reference module;
- current promotion or audit report when needed.

Do not reread the entire world for local edits. Run whole-world audits from registries, summaries, comparison tables, and selected evidence, then inspect flagged subjects deeply.

## Process compaction

Periodically compact process history without altering canon:

- preserve current canonical subjects and registries;
- preserve user approval, canon locks, replacements, major scope decisions, and unresolved obligations;
- summarize closed iteration logs by decision and affected IDs;
- remove redundant exploratory wording and rejected hypotheses with no reusable lesson;
- retain audit artifacts required for the current completion claim;
- keep `state.yaml`, queues, ledgers, and registry summaries small enough to resume reliably.

Compaction must not erase why a user-approved claim changed, why a subject was replaced, or which evidence supports completion.

## Work packet

Every autonomous unit states:

```yaml
id: work.magic.mass_use
level: 2
phase: deepen
target: world.subject.magic_foundation
reason: Current rules describe individual casting only.
inputs:
  - world.subject.magic_foundation
  - world.subject.royal_army
done_when:
  - mass-use interaction is decided
  - infrastructure consequences are integrated or explicitly rejected
  - new scope obligations are queued
```

Close one packet before opening too many new ones. New discoveries enter queues rather than interrupting every active packet.

## Priority

Prioritize by load-bearing impact, blocked dependencies, explanation debt, reader-facing importance, contradiction risk, and maturity promotion value. Do not prioritize easy node creation over difficult structural work.

## Plateau recovery

If repeated cycles produce the same failure:

1. stop generating siblings;
2. challenge the governing premise or taxonomy;
3. inspect rescue cascades and semantic saturation;
4. rescope, merge, replace, or discard the source;
5. change the expansion strategy before resuming.

## Interruption and resumption

Before yielding because of environment limits, persist the exact active packet, last accepted decisions, affected subjects, open obligations, and next action. On resume, continue from state rather than restarting ideation.

## User checkpoints

User approval is required for changes to supplied canon, explicit exclusions, or a major creative fork whose branches cannot be reconciled. Routine generated material remains provisional until internal review; the agent may replace it autonomously.
