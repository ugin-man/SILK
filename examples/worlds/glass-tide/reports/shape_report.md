# Shape report

## World signature

- Intended appeals: tactile maritime work, restrained wonder, competent mutual aid, and disputed material history.
- Load-bearing questions: What can a perishable extraordinary material change without becoming universal infrastructure? How does a settlement organize care when risk is regular but authority is local?
- Rhythms: forty-minute dawn harvests, daily decay, neighborhood watch rotations, and a six-to-nine-week route season.
- Cohesion: shared coast, material dependence, seasonal access, labor adaptation, and compatible restrained tone.
- Independence protected: the Pale Current has an oceanographic cause; the Rain Vault has an inland rain-recording history and no accepted relations.

## Topology

Five relations form a small coastal cluster around First Light without a universal supernode. Ember Tide has two relations, First Light three, Cooling Houses two, Ash-Divers two, Pale Current one, and Rain Vault none. Degree is not the success measure: every accepted edge changes behavior on both ends, while three likely but empty links are recorded as rejected.

## Counterfactual checks

Removing the Ember Tide would change the world's main identity, but the Pale Current and Rain Vault would still have independent reasons to exist. Moving the Rain Vault unchanged into another setting would lose its relative chronology and deliberate contrast with coastal claims, yet no false mechanism is added merely to bind it to glass.
