# Interest audit

## Verdict

Keep the six-subject compact world for human review. The strongest element is the direct link from a beautiful but perishable material to concrete work, care, and storage decisions. The strongest weakness is that the Rain Vault is intentionally light and may feel like an audit device until a human judges whether its quiet independent history is evocative enough.

## Artifact-first findings

- Ember Tide realizes tactile wonder through a visible dawn skin, scoring, decay, and failed stockpiling rather than through a list of powers.
- First Light makes the premise inhabitable through lane closure, ordinary imports, neighborhood responsibility, and seasonal warehousing.
- Cooling Houses offer an attractive canopy meal and competent care without hiding a conspiracy or claiming utopia.
- Ash-Divers are neither chosen heroes nor immune masters; expertise changes work and can reduce earning capacity.
- Pale Current provides a stable, ordinary system whose cause and limitations do not repeat heat memory.
- Rain Vault gives the world chronological evidence beyond the premise, but its low connection density is a deliberate risk rather than automatically interesting.

## Rejected alternative

`The Furnace Crown` would have made a single order control heat glass, origin teaching, trade, care, and regional law. Its apparent clarity created a semantic monopoly and large rescue debt. It was rejected rather than repaired with exceptions.
