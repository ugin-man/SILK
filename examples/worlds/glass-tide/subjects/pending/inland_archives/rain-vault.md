# Rain Vault

The Rain Vault lies inland beyond the chalk watershed. Its roof channels rainfall through tuned stone throats into sealed jars. Changes in pitch tell keepers which channel is filling; sediment and mineral rings turn each jar into a long seasonal record. The archive's oldest lower channels were sealed before the first coastal harvest marks appear in traded stone.

Keepers copy summaries onto dry clay tablets, but the water jars remain the primary evidence. Drought creates blank intervals rather than hidden messages. A cracked throat can merge two storms and must be marked as damaged. The practice is material and local, not prophecy and not another form of heat memory.

The Vault is deliberately outside the coastal relation graph. It does not receive the Pale Current, owe service to a cooling house, employ ash-divers, or explain the Ember Tide. Coastal historians may use its chronology, but that possible research use is not an accepted subject relation. Its separate rain-listening tradition demonstrates that the compact world has history not generated from glass.
